## **The Night of Falling Stars - Death of the Twilight Court**

_"Some victories taste of ash and starlight. This one tasted of both."_  
— High Commander Aelindra Starweaver

---

### **Date:**

**November 15th-16th, Year 99 of Duskfall** – Near the end of the Mythrendil Civil War

---

### **Duration:**

One night of concentrated assault and sacrifice

---

### **Location:**

- **Shadowspire Hold** – Corrupted fortress-temple of the Twilight Court
- **The Void Chamber** – Central ritual space of the Twilight leadership
- **The Shadow Paths** – Ancient teleportation network corrupted by dark magic
- **The Star Dome** – Once-sacred astronomical observatory turned dark ritual site

---

### **Background:**

After seven years of civil war, the elven people were bleeding to death by inches. Though the Twilight Faction had failed to take Lorianthil, their shadow magic continued to corrupt more territory and turn more elves to their cause. **High Commander Aelindra Starweaver** realized that without decisive action, the war of attrition would eventually doom them all.

Through the visions of **Seeress Lirael Starbrook**, the loyalists discovered a critical weakness: during the lunar eclipse of the Long Night, **Shadowlord Vaerien Nightweaver** and his inner circle would conduct a grand ritual to permanently corrupt the Shadow Paths, giving them unprecedented mobility throughout elven territories.

The timing was impossible - their forces were stretched thin defending multiple fronts. The cost would be catastrophic - any assault would face the full might of the Twilight Court's elite guard. But the alternative was slow extinction. Aelindra gathered her most skilled warriors for what many knew would be a one-way mission.

### **The Battle – Hour by Hour:**

---

**First Watch – The Approach**  
_"We walked paths of starlight while darkness watched."_  
— Spellblade Vaerion Duskwind

As the eclipse began, Seeress Lirael led the ritual to open forgotten paths of pure starlight - ancient routes that bypassed the corrupted Shadow Paths. Through these celestial bridges, Aelindra's forces infiltrated the outer reaches of Shadowspire Hold.

The first casualties came silently, as shadow wards detected some of the infiltrators. Thirty elves simply ceased to exist, their bodies dispersed into shadow. But their sacrifices revealed safe paths for the others.

---

**Second Watch – The Binding**  
_"Every star above was an anchor, every life below a chain."_  
— Seeress Lirael Starbrook

While the Twilight Court focused on their grand ritual, the Starfall Archers took position in the highest towers. Using arrows blessed with pure starlight, they began the binding ritual - each shot anchoring a point of light that would temporarily suppress shadow magic.

The cost was terrible. Each arrow required the life force of its archer to power the binding. One by one, the archers collapsed, their bodies turning to stardust as their arrows flew true. But their sacrifice created a net of light that trapped the shadow within.

---

**Third Watch – The Breach**  
_"They thought shadows their allies. We showed them true darkness."_  
— High Commander Aelindra Starweaver

With shadow magic partially suppressed, the Spellblades launched their assault. **Vaerion Duskwind** led the charge into the Void Chamber, his enchanted blade cutting through shadow guards like light through darkness.

The fighting was brutal and intimate. Shadow Guards could still phase through solid matter, while Void Weavers unmade reality around them. But without their full power, they couldn't stop the determined assault of elves who had come prepared to die.

---

**Final Watch – The Sundering**  
_"His shadow broke. His power broke. Everything broke."_  
— Grove Warden Survivor

In the Star Dome, Aelindra herself confronted Shadowlord Vaerien. Their duel shattered reality around them as shadow magic clashed with pure starlight. The Grove Wardens began their own ritual, using the power of nature itself to purge shadow from stone.

Vaerien, realizing his defeat was at hand, attempted to unmake the entire fortress. But Seeress Lirael had foreseen this. The remaining Spellblades formed a circle, linking their life force to contain the explosion. Their combined sacrifice created a barrier that turned Vaerien's death curse back upon himself and his inner circle.

The Shadowlord's death scream shattered every window in the fortress. When it faded, only forty-three of the attacking force remained alive.

The Night of Falling Stars marked the beginning of the end for the Mythrendil Civil War. Though the Twilight Faction would continue to fight under lesser leaders, their power was broken. The cost had been tremendous, but the sacrifice of the three hundred prevented an even darker fate for all elven kind.

To this day, on the anniversary of the battle, lights can be seen floating above Shadowspire Hold - said to be the spirits of the fallen, still standing guard against the return of shadow.
