# Emberfall Legion Military Command Structure

*"Command is a burden carried in blood and sealed in steel."*  
— General Darius Crownhold

## Command Hierarchy

### Level 1 - Supreme Command
**Lord Commander**
- Current: Aldric Blackthorn
- Commands: Entire Legion (All Forces)
- Location: High Keep, Emberfall
- Responsibilities:
  - Supreme military authority
  - Final strategic decisions
  - Legion policy and doctrine
  - High-level diplomatic relations

### Level 2 - High Command
**General**
- Example: General Darius Crownhold
- Commands: Theater Army (5,000-10,000)
- Primary Units: Multiple Battalions
- Responsibilities:
  - Theater-wide strategy
  - Resource allocation
  - Campaign planning
  - Coordination with Council of War

### Level 3 - Regional Command
**Warden**
- Example: Warden Alric Stoneveil
- Commands: Fortress/District Forces (1,000-3,000)
- Primary Units: Key Installation Defense Forces
- Responsibilities:
  - Fortress/district defense
  - Local resource management
  - Civilian administration
  - Infrastructure security

### Level 4 - Unit Command
**Captain**
- Example: Captain Isolde Veyne
- Commands: Company (500-1,000)
- Primary Units: Multiple Platoons
- Responsibilities:
  - Tactical operations
  - Unit training
  - Supply management
  - Mission execution

### Level 5 - Squad Command
**Lieutenant**
- Commands: Platoon (100-500)
- Primary Units: Multiple Squads
- Responsibilities:
  - Direct combat leadership
  - Tactical implementation
  - Unit cohesion
  - Mission supervision

**Sergeant**
- Commands: Squad (20-100)
- Primary Units: Individual Teams
- Responsibilities:
  - Direct soldier supervision
  - Basic unit training
  - Combat leadership
  - Morale maintenance

### Base Level
**Legionnaire**
- Basic military unit
- Part of: Squad/Team
- Responsibilities:
  - Combat duties
  - Guard assignments
  - Patrol operations
  - Basic maintenance

**Initiate**
- Training status
- Part of: Training Cohort
- Responsibilities:
  - Combat training
  - Physical conditioning
  - Basic duties
  - Skill development

## Unit Organization

### Theater Army
- Commanded by: General
- Size: 5,000-10,000
- Components:
  - Multiple Battalions
  - Support Units
  - Specialist Detachments
  - Logistics Corps

### Fortress/District Force
- Commanded by: Warden
- Size: 1,000-3,000
- Components:
  - Garrison Units
  - Defense Forces
  - Support Staff
  - Civil Guard

### Company
- Commanded by: Captain
- Size: 500-1,000
- Components:
  - Combat Platoons
  - Support Teams
  - Specialist Units
  - Supply Section

### Platoon
- Commanded by: Lieutenant
- Size: 100-500
- Components:
  - Combat Squads
  - Support Elements
  - Special Teams

### Squad
- Commanded by: Sergeant
- Size: 20-100
- Components:
  - Combat Teams
  - Specialist Members
  - Support Personnel

## Command Protocols

### Chain of Command
1. Orders flow down through ranks
2. Reports flow up through ranks
3. Skip-echelon command only in emergencies
4. Each level responsible for subordinate units

### Command Authority
1. Direct authority over assigned units
2. Tactical control in area of operations
3. Emergency authority as needed
4. Coordination with parallel units

### Responsibility Scope
1. Strategic (General and above)
2. Operational (Warden/Captain)
3. Tactical (Lieutenant/Sergeant)
4. Direct Action (Squad Level)

## Special Considerations

### Combat Status Integration
- Affects tactical authority
- Influences unit assignments
- Provides additional respect
- Grants tactical privileges

### Cross-Path Coordination
- With Inquisitorial Forces
- With War-Mage Units
- With Specialist Divisions
- With Support Services

### Emergency Protocols
- Chain of Command disruption
- battlefield promotions
- Unit consolidation
- Emergency command
